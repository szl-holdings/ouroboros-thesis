# Consent to participate — Trace-Aided Audit Review study

You are being invited to participate in a research study conducted by SZL Holdings (Stephen P. Lutar Jr., Principal Investigator). Please read this form carefully before agreeing.

## What the study is about

We are studying whether access to a **convergence trace** alongside an AI agent's final decision changes how reviewers evaluate that decision. You will review 8 short scenarios. For 4 of them, you will see the final decision and a minimal context. For the other 4, you will see the same plus a step-by-step trace. We will measure your time, your confidence, and how often you correctly identify deliberately-seeded errors.

## What we will ask you to do

- Complete a 5-minute primer on the trace format.
- Review 8 scenarios, answering 4 short questions per scenario (Yes/No, free text, a 1–7 confidence rating, and what evidence helped most).
- Participate in a 3-minute debrief at the end.

Total time: about 30 minutes. You can pause and resume.

## Risks

This study presents minimal risk. The scenarios are anonymized — there is no personally identifying information about anyone other than you. Some scenarios contain deliberately seeded errors; this is part of the study design.

## Benefits

You will receive a $50 gift card or charity donation in your name on completion. You will also receive a copy of the published paper if you wish.

## Confidentiality

- Your responses are tied to a randomly-generated session ID, not your name.
- We collect your email address only to send compensation; the email is stored separately from response data and deleted within 30 days of compensation distribution.
- Aggregated, anonymized results may appear in a published paper. No quote will be attributed to you without separate written consent.
- Raw data is retained in encrypted storage for 12 months, then permanently deleted.

## Voluntary participation

Participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time, for any reason, without penalty. If you withdraw, your responses up to that point will be deleted at your request.

## Conflicts of interest

This study is conducted by SZL Holdings, the developer of the Ouroboros runtime being evaluated. The PI has a clear interest in a positive result. The study design includes a pre-registered null hypothesis and a commitment to report null findings. The protocol and analysis plan are publicly archived prior to data collection.
